**Company:** NYT Games

### Challenge

I joined NYT Games during hyper-growth following the Wordle acquisition, with millions of new casual players entering the ecosystem.

Before Wordle, users were primarily news readers who played Crosswords—the pinnacle of puzzle games—and a traditional anchor for subscriptions. After Wordle, the influx of new audiences was more casual and less attracted to Crosswords.

The business model was simple: play the daily game for free, and subscribe to access Crosswords and all levels in Spelling Bee. But the daily puzzle had a fundamental limitation: a 24‑hour shelf life. After that, it was gone.

With millions of casual Wordle players, the strategic question became: “Come for Wordle, stay for…?”


### Framework For Growth

Our goal was to increase player engagement by moving players down the funnel to the most habituated surfaces and deepening their relationship with NYT Games. This was a multi-dimensional strategy, set by the VP of Product, that mapped the user journey across three axes:

- **Monetization** – Convert free players → registered users → paying subscribers **(my responsibility)**  
- **Engagement depth** – Convert low-engaged → mid-engaged → high-engaged players **(my responsibility)**  
- **Platform** – Convert mobile web + desktop users → Games app **(partially owned)**


### Approach

Build **interconnected value pillars** that compound over time and work across the entire portfolio.

**1. Puzzle Archives – Maximize ROI for Every Human‑Crafted Puzzle**

Launched full archives for Wordle, Connections, Spelling Bee, and Strands, expanding our core value proposition to the most engaged games beyond Crossword.  Transformed 24‑hour shelf-life puzzles into permanent, playable libraries.  Extended delight and allowed players to catch up on missed puzzles.  Archives becomes the leading **subscriber benefits**.

> Impact
> - Drive subscriber growth **212%** for Games-only subscribers from 2023 to 2025.
> - Increase conversion **Wordle Archive:** XXXX incremental weekly starts; ~10.4% of Games starts attributed to the archive.  **Connections Archive:** XXX incremental weekly starts; ~2.5% of Games-only starts attributed to the archive.  
> - Increase player engagement : **Spelling Bee Archive:** +10% active days for subscribers.  

### 2. Stats & Streaks – Modernize Progress-Tracking Infrastructure 

Advocated for and partnered with the platform team to fully migrate millions of records of player game progress from client-side to server-side.  Introduced the concept of **game-agnostic metrics** alongside **game-specific stats**, setting the foundation for durable progress tracking.  Created the infrastructure needed for achievements and metagame mechanics that deepen player habituation.

> Impact
> Increase registrations: - **Connections Stats:** ~14K incremental registrations/week.  **Strands Stats:** ~2K incremental registrations/week.

### 3. Achievement System – Celebrate Wins and Capture Memories
Led a 2-year strategy to build a dynamic achievement system that drives deeper engagement across a range of player types.  Designed earning paths for casual to hardcore players: all-in completionists, gregarious sharers, collectors, and overachievers.  Introduced time-limited badge drops for special moments (annual events, personal milestones) to create FOMO and memorable experiences.

> Impact
> Increase 30-day retention lift by +0.36%

### What I Learned

- **Compounding systems beat isolated features for retention.**  
Stats drive archive play; archives unlock achievements; achievements create new stats to track. Each pillar reinforces the others, creating durable value that algorithm changes can’t easily disrupt.

- **Game studio "waterfall" culture requires ongoing advocacy.**  
In organizations that are naturally focused on polished product, rather than build > test > iterate, I   work hard to secure buy-in prove of the value experimentation and MVP, and do math to demonstrate incremental impact to justify continued infrastructure investment while navigating constraints and competing priorities.

- **Execution reality: build a scalable foundation.**  
  Each iteration taught us how to build faster, better, and more scalable—creating a turnkey foundation that drives higher ROI with each subsequent game we plug into the system.
